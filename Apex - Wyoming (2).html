The Process

Twelve commissions, four seasons.

We work the way a Wyoming ranch works. A long brief, an unhurried recce, a shoot that respects weather and wildlife windows. Every step authored by the same director who signed your initial proposal.

i.

Brief & recce

Days 1 — 7

A site visit, a conversation with the architect and the ranch manager when possible, and a one-page treatment that fixes the film's tone before any camera arrives.

ii.

Light & season study

Days 7 — 21

We track the sun, the snow line and the cottonwood color across the property. We do not shoot the house — we shoot the house's best ninety minutes of the year.

iii.

Principal photography

Days 21 — 24

Three days on site. Aerial, ground and interior captured by one unified team, one color science, one director making decisions in real time around weather and wildlife.

iv.

Edit & finish

Days 24 — 42

An assembly within seven days, a director's cut within fourteen, color and delivery by day forty-two. Two rounds of notes. No third.
